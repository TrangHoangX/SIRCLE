Welcome to S-IRCLE project


S-IRCLE project focuses on addressing key challenges in small-scale biogas plants. The primary objective is to develop an innovative, automatic, regenerable external filter system capable of effectively removing hydrogen sulfide (H2S) from biogas. This is crucial as H2S poses health risks and can lead to corrosion, reducing the lifespan of biogas appliances. Furthermore, the project aims to recycle sulfur present in the loaded filter material as an alternative sulfur fertilizer, promoting sustainable resource utilization.

Overall, S-IRCLE project encompasses both technical development and environmental evaluation, aiming to contribute to the advancement of biogas technology in Vietnam and pave the way for a more sustainable and efficient biogas utilization.

Investigate and taking samples from the large-scale biogas plant in Dong Thap province

01/04/2018

With the support of Biogas Viet company, the scientist group from Germany had visited a large-scale biogas plant in Dong Thap province. The size of the biogas plant is more than 20000m3, constructed with HDPE material. This plant starts working from 11/2017. Currently, the biogas from the plant is all burnt by using a gas flare.

The excursion to biogas plants in the South of Vietnam

27/03/2018

In the second day of the workshop, the participants joined an excursion to visit the 2 biogas plants in medium and large scale in Binh Duong province. Both bio plants are designed and constructed by APO CORP. using HDPE material. Pig manure is the feedstock for the two biogas plants. In one biogas plant, the power generators are used and generate electricity from the biogas. The excursion was helpful and gave the participants a closer look at the mid- and large-scale biogas plants in Vietnam.

Workshop "Environmental and climate-friendly adaptation of biogas plants in Vietnam" took place at Ho Chi Minh city, Vietnam

26/03/2018

The UKAVita project team in collaboration with HCMC University of Science has successfully organized the workshop "Environmental and climate-friendly adaptation of biogas plants in Vietnam" with the participation of many biogas companies from Vietnam and Germany and the professors, scientists coming from different universities in the South of Vietnam. In the first part of the workshop, the presentations had shown the development of biogas technology in Germany and the status-quo and the trend of the biogas plants in Vietnam. In the second part, the interacive session was done within the groups in order to develop business models from creative ideas using biogas technology, dicuss about the challanges and solutions.
